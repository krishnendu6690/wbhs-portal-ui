About Us

The Medical Cell, functioning under the Finance Department (Group-P2), Government of West Bengal, administers the West Bengal Health Scheme (WBHS). It is responsible for policy implementation, empanelment of hospitals, approval of specialized treatments and settlement of medical claims.

Functions of the Medical Cell
1. Policy Administration

Issues operational guidelines, circulars and clarifications for the effective implementation of the West Bengal Health Scheme.

2. Approval of Specialized Treatments
  • Multiple procedures exceeding ₹50,000.
  • Drug Eluting Stents.
  • AICD / CRT with AICD.
  • Deep Brain Stimulator.
  • Cochlear Implant.
  • Spinal Cord Stimulator.
  • Chemotherapy / Immunotherapy approvals.
  • High-value Non-Package Treatments.
3. Treatment Outside the State

Prior consultation with WBHSA is mandatory before granting permission for treatment in speciality hospitals outside West Bengal, except in specified cases.

4. Standardization of Rates

Fixes and revises approved rates for medical procedures and investigations from time to time.

5. Empanelment of Healthcare Providers

Empanels hospitals, nursing homes, diagnostic centres and laboratories and classifies them according to available facilities.

6. Cashless Claim Settlement

Scrutinizes claims submitted by empanelled hospitals, settles eligible claims and issues CARC for admissible reimbursement beyond the prescribed limit.

7. Advisory Role

Acts as an advisory authority for other health schemes implemented by the Government of West Bengal.
