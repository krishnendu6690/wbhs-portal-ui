About Us

The Medical Cell, functioning under the Finance Department (Group-P2), Government of West Bengal, administers the West Bengal Health Scheme (WBHS). It is responsible for policy implementation, empanelment of hospitals, approval of specialized treatments and settlement of medical claims.

Functions of the Medical Cell
1. Policy Administration

Medical Cell in consultation with the Health and Family Welfare Department, wherever necessary, issues operational guidelines, clarifications etc. for implementation of the West Bengal Health Scheme

2. Approval of Specialized Treatments
  • More than 2 procedures where total cost exceeds Rs. 50,000/-.
  • Implantation of more than 2 (two) Drug Eluting Stent.
  • Implantation of AICD.
  • Implantation of CRT with AICD.
  • Implantation of Deep Brain Stimulator.
  • Implantation of Intra-Thecal Pumps.
  • Implantation of Cochlear.
  • Implantation of Spinal Cord Stimulator.
  • Chemotherapy / Immunotherapy where medicine cost exceeds Rs. 50,000/- per cycle (once for entire chemo schedules).
  • Non-package treatment whenever treatment cost exceeds Rs. 5,00,000/- for Tata Medical Center, Rajarhat and Rs. 3,50,000/- for other private empanelled hospitals.
  • Successive permission after every 10 (ten) days against a permitted non-packaged treatment whenever treatment cost exceeds Rs. 5,00,000/- for Tata Medical Center, Rajarhat and Rs. 3,50,000/- for other private empanelled hospitals.
3. Treatment Outside the State

It is compulsory for any Department to consult The West Bengal Health Scheme Authority (WBHSA), apex policy making body of the Medical Cell before according permission for treatment in Speciality Hospital outside the State subject to certain exceptions.

4. Standardization of Rates

Medical Cell fixes the approved rate for various surgical procedures and investigations from time to time.

5. Empanelment of Healthcare Providers

It is empowered to empanel Private Hospitals, Nursing Homes, Institutions, Clinics, Laboratories, and Diagnostic Centres within the state and classify them into Class-1 and Class-2 service provider on the basis of facilities available there.

6. Cashless Claim Settlement

In case of cashless treatment, the HCOs submit the whole bill to the Medical Cell. Medical Cell checks and fixes the admissibility of the claim submitted by the HCOs and makes payment of HCO’s claim upto ₹ 2,00,000.00. It also issues CARC for the reimbursement of the claim of the Employee/ Pensioner over and above HCO’s claim of ₹ 2,00,000.00.

7. Advisory Role

Medical cell acts as an advisory body in respect of any other Health Scheme implemented by the Govt. of West Bengal.
